Chlamydia A very common STI, often shows no symptoms, and spreads through vaginal and anal intercourse. It is treatable with antibiotics but can lead to Infertility and Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID), especially in women. Trichomoniasis An treatable infection caused by Trichomonas Vaginalis Parasite and contracted through sexual contact. Often shows no symptoms but causes a strong-smelling discharge, itching, and painful urination in women.
Gonorrhea A frequent bacterial infection in human's rectum, throat, or eyes, which occurs as a result of sexual contact with fluids. Symptoms are asymptomatic. Needs antibiotics to avoid having complications such as Infertility or Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID). Scabies A skin condition caused by mites that infest the human skin by extensive skin-to-skin contact and sexual intercourse that shows extreme itching sensations as well as manifestation of an itching rash and mite burrows that infest into the human skin as it lays eggs.
Syphilis Caused by a bacterium referred to as the Spirochaete Treponema Pallidum and can only propagate via sexual routes. The manifestations are normally fourth-stage. Can attack all the organs in the body, and been referred as the "Great Imitator" because of the divergent signs and manifestations, including a painless sore referred to as a chancre. It can be easily treated. Pubic Lice (Crabs) A tiny crab-like parasites that infest thick hair on the body and feed on the blood of humans. These parasites are most commonly found in the pubic area and cause intense itching.
HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) A virus that attacks the body's immune system. If not treated properly, it may cause AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome). It is acquired through unprotected intercourse that can introduce the viruses in the blood, semen before orgasm, vaginal fluids, or fluids found in the rectal area into the human body. Amebiasis and Giardiasis An intestinal infection caused by the Protozoan Parasite Entamoeba Histolytica. Spreads through oral-anal or oral-genital contact and particularly common among men who have sex with men.
